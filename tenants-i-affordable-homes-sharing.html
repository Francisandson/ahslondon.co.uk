Your questions answered

Amenities

What is provided for bedroom furniture?

As a minimum you will always have a bed, bedside cabinet and/or drawers and wardrobe. (At an additional cost we also offer starter packs which have brand new sheets, pillows, a duvet and a towel set).

Is there a living room?

We want to make sure our prices to be the most competitive on the market therefore our property do not offer separate living room. However, as we are aware of the importance of proper space for social relationships and private relaxation, we try our best to ensure that the majority of our rooms have its own sitting/relaxing area with chair/bin bags/armchair or sofa and adequate space in the kitchen for communal dining area.

Is there a living room?

Yes.

We always provide adequate space for communal dining area with table and chairs.

Do you provide all kitchenware?

Yes.

We provide everything you need including cutlery, crockery, utensils, pots and pans, a kettle, a microwave and other essential items.

How is the space shared in the kitchen and fridge?

We are aware of the problems arising when sharing a property therefore we apply labels with rooms’ numbers on cupboards and fridges.

What about an iron and ironing board?

Yes.
We provide ironing equipment in every house.

Cleaning

How is the flat maintained cleaned?

We offer a free professional weekly cleaning service for the communal areas of the property.

However, every tenant has to wash and put away its on dishes, throw rubbish away and keep the communal area tidy and clean in respect of others flatmates.

Is my room cleaned prior to me moving in?

Yes.

Every single time anyone leaves a property we organise for an extra deep clean.

Do you charge for cleaning when we leave?

Yes, we ask a small fee (currently between £25 and £35) for cleaning when you leave.

Can I do my own end of tenancy clean to save on the fee?

Sure, as long your definition of clean is up to our standards.

Choosing tenants

What sort of people rent your properties?

Our tenants are working corporate and professionals of any age from 20’s to 60’s. We try our best to group tenants of similar age in the same property.

Can we decide who moves in if a room comes available in our house?

We are happy to give you every opportunity to choose who you live with, however we will need to approve of any potential tenant prior to moving in.

Do you accept couples?

Yes, but not in every property. 

Usually there is a limit of one per property unless it has extra facilities.

Is there a limit to the amount of people you put into a property?

Yes, absolutely. 

We don’t expect you to live in an overcrowded house so we are mindful about how many people live in each property.

Can I have a friend stay?

Yes, of course. 

It is your home and you can have friends visit, but only for a short period of time. We also need to be made aware of anyone extra staying as a guest for safety and insurance reasons (just send us an email).

Deposit

How much deposit is required to hold the room from the moment of the viewing until the moving date?

A 1 week holding deposit is required in order to secure the room for you. This means that from the moment we receive the holding deposit we remove the advert from the web and we stop doing further viewings.

This also means that you will not be required to pay the first week of your rent once you move in.

How much security deposit is required to be paid and when?

In relation to the type of contract used It may vary from a minimum of 2 weeks to a maximum of 1 month.

The security deposit has to be paid on the day the contract is signed and keys are handed over.

Can I pay my last rent via my deposit?

No.

A deposit is kept to cover any damages in the property or any unpaid rent; you cannot use it to pay rent.

Maintenance and Repairs

Whom do I call if there is something that needs to be fixed in the property?

You call our team who will organise a contractor to fix any issues.

What if it is an emergency and you are closed?

Prior to moving in, we give you numbers of our contractors you can call out of office hours if there is a genuine emergency.

Do we have to stay home to provide access to tradesmen?

We try whenever possible to provide keys and access ourselves, but on the odd occasion we may request that you provide access.

Rent

Are all the bills included in the rent?

Yes.

All bills, council tax, wi-fi, and weekly cleaner.

We also include toilet paper, kitchen paper, kitchen towels, hand soap, dishwashing soap and sponges!

Is there any extra cost associated with the internet?

No. We cover the costs ourselves.

How can I pay the rent?

You can pay upfront rent weekly, fortnightly or monthly.

Do you do any discounts?

Yes!

We really value you as special customer therefore we have decided to give you 1 free day rent on the day of your birthday!

What are your fees and charges?

No Fees and 0% Commission.

If requested we only charge for starter pack which contains brand new sheets, pillows, a duvet and a towel set.

Tenancy Contract

Would I sign any contract?

Yes, you would sign an agreement which will protect your and our rights.

Can I keep a copy?

Yes absolutely!

We will email you a signed copy so you will always have one to hand.

What if I decide to leave?

During the last month of the fixed term (and not later than 14 days before the end of the fixed term), you must tell us whether you intend to stay in the property or leave at the end of the fixed term.

Once the fixed term is ended in relation to the type of contract used you will need to give between 2 weeks and 1 month written notice prior living.

About Affordable home sharing

Are you estate agents?

No.

Affordable Home Sharing is a property manager. We sign a fixed term management agreement with you (usually 3-5 years) to take charge of your property.

• During that time, we pay you a guaranteed rent, every month, regardless of the property’s’ occupancy.
• We organise the maintenance of your property.
• Finding and checking references of our corporate and professional tenants, keeping them happy in their homes, and collecting their rent, becomes Affordable Home Sharing’s responsibility.
